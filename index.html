A quieter place to meet yourself

Moonspace is an online counselling practice for people who feel mentally tired, emotionally overwhelmed, or unsure where to begin. It offers a calm and steady space to talk, reflect, and slowly understand what is happening within you.

Therapy here is not rushed. There is room to pause, to speak freely, and to move at a pace that feels manageable.

Why Moonspace exists

Many people carry a lot quietly. Stress builds up, thoughts loop, emotions get pushed aside because life keeps moving. Reaching out for therapy can feel daunting, especially when it seems clinical or overwhelming.

Moonspace was created to feel different. It is meant to feel like stepping into a calmer room, where you do not need to explain everything perfectly or have clear answers. Just showing up is enough.

This space may be right for you if

You feel anxious, restless, or mentally overloaded.

You are experiencing burnout or emotional exhaustion.

You struggle with overthinking or feeling stuck.

You are new to therapy and want a gentle start.

You are looking for online counselling in India that feels personal.

Online counselling services

Moonspace offers individual online counselling sessions for adults. Sessions take place through secure video calls and are fully confidential.

Therapy can support you with anxiety, stress, burnout, emotional regulation, self-esteem concerns, and periods of transition. Sessions are shaped around what feels most relevant to you rather than a fixed agenda.

How therapy is approached

Therapy at Moonspace is collaborative and reflective. It is not about being told what to do or rushing towards solutions. Instead, it is a space to notice patterns, understand emotions, and gently build clarity over time.

The work focuses on helping you feel more grounded, more aware, and more able to respond to life rather than feeling overwhelmed by it.

Your therapist

Girisha Kamboj is a trained mental health professional offering online counselling in India. Her work is rooted in ethical, evidence-based practice and a deep respect for each person’s lived experience.

She believes therapy works best when it feels safe and unhurried. Sessions focus on listening carefully, understanding emotional experiences, and creating space for meaningful change to develop.
