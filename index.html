A quieter place to meet yourself

Moonspace is an online counselling practice for people who feel mentally tired, emotionally overwhelmed, or unsure where to begin. It offers a calm and steady space to talk, reflect, and slowly understand what is happening within you.

Therapy here is not rushed. There is room to pause, to speak freely, and to move at a pace that feels manageable.

Why Moonspace exists

Moonspace was created in response to how overwhelming modern life can quietly become. Many people carry anxiety, exhaustion, self-doubt, and emotional pain while still functioning, achieving, and appearing “fine” on the outside. There is often little room to slow down, reflect, or be met with patience rather than solutions.

Moonspace exists to offer that pause.

It is a space designed for people who feel stretched thin, emotionally cluttered, or disconnected from themselves, yet may not know how to articulate it. A space where experiences are explored gently, without urgency to fix or label, and without pressure to perform progress.

The name Moonspace reflects this intention. Much like the moon, which does not rush or compete with the sun, this space honours quieter rhythms. It acknowledges phases, uncertainty, and the parts of us that surface only when things are calm enough to be seen. Moonspace exists to support reflection, emotional regulation, and deeper self-understanding, at a pace that feels safe and respectful.

Online counselling services

Moonspace offers individual online counselling sessions for adults. Sessions take place through secure video calls and are fully confidential.

Therapy can support you with anxiety, stress, burnout, emotional regulation, self-esteem concerns, and periods of transition. Sessions are shaped around what feels most relevant to you rather than a fixed agenda.

How therapy is approached

Therapy at Moonspace is approached as a collaborative and relational process, not a prescriptive one. There is no rigid agenda imposed on your experiences, and no expectation that healing should look a certain way or follow a timeline.

Sessions focus on creating psychological safety first. This means paying close attention to emotional boundaries, comfort levels, and the nervous system. Conversations unfold at a pace that allows insight without overwhelm, and exploration without intrusion.

The therapeutic work draws from established psychological frameworks while remaining flexible to individual needs. Emphasis is placed on understanding patterns, emotional responses, relational dynamics, and internal narratives, rather than simply managing symptoms. Reflection, emotional processing, and meaning-making are central to the work.

Importantly, therapy here is not about being “fixed.” It is about developing awareness, compassion, and agency, so that clients feel more grounded within themselves and better equipped to navigate their lives with clarity and self-trust.

Online sessions are conducted with the same depth, presence, and ethical care as in-person therapy, offering continuity, accessibility, and privacy.

Your therapist

Moonspace is led by Girisha Kamboj, a psychologist who values depth, nuance, and ethical care over quick interventions. Her therapeutic approach is rooted in curiosity, empathy, and respect for each individual’s lived experience.

Girisha’s training and clinical education have emphasized not only psychological theory and technique, but also self-reflection, boundaries, and the responsibility that comes with holding another person’s inner world. This informs a practice that is thoughtful, emotionally attuned, and grounded in professional ethics.

She works particularly well with individuals navigating anxiety, emotional overwhelm, identity questions, burnout, and relational difficulties. Sessions are shaped by careful listening rather than assumptions, allowing clients to feel seen without being reduced to diagnoses or labels.

Above all, Girisha believes that therapy should feel human. It should allow space for uncertainty, complexity, and growth, while maintaining clear structure, confidentiality, and care.
